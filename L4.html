History

Santorini’s history is shaped by a massive volcanic eruption around 1600 BCE that created its dramatic caldera. It was home to the advanced Minoan civilization, whose ruins can still be seen at Akrotiri.

Culture

Santorini reflects traditional Greek island culture, with whitewashed houses and blue-domed churches. Local wine-making, seafood cuisine, and sunset traditions are central to island life.

Location

Santorini is located in the southern Aegean Sea, part of Greece’s Cyclades islands. It sits about 200 km southeast of mainland Greece.

Why it Matters

Santorini matters for its unique volcanic landscape and rich ancient history. It is one of the world’s most iconic travel destinations, symbolizing Greek island beauty.
