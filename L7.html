History

Portofino began as a small fishing village known to the Romans as “Portus Delphini.” It grew over centuries as a quiet harbor for fishermen and traders along the Ligurian coast. By the 19th century, it attracted European aristocrats and artists seeking its beauty. Today, it is a world-famous luxury destination while preserving its historic charm.

Culture

Portofino is known for its elegant coastal lifestyle and Mediterranean charm. It attracts artists, celebrities, and travelers inspired by its colorful harbor and relaxed atmosphere. The town blends Italian traditions, seafood cuisine, and luxury tourism with timeless beauty.

Location

Nestled along the Italian Riviera, Portofino sits between lush hills and the sparkling Ligurian Sea.

Why it Matters

Portofino blends natural beauty with luxury and heritage, making it one of Italy’s most iconic seaside destinations.
