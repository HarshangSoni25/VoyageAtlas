History

Lauterbrunnen was first mentioned in 1240 and began as a small Alpine farming village. Its name means “many fountains,” referring to the valley’s waterfalls. In the 19th century, it became popular with travelers during the rise of Alpine tourism.

Culture

Its culture is rooted in traditional mountain life, with wooden chalets, local festivals, and strong Swiss Alpine traditions.

Location

Lauterbrunnen is located in the Bernese Oberland of Switzerland, surrounded by steep Alpine cliffs and waterfalls.

Why it Matters

Lauterbrunnen is one of the most stunning glacier-carved valleys in Europe, symbolizing the dramatic natural beauty of the Swiss Alps.
